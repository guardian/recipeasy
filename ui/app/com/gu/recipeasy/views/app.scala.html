What you need to do

Step 1
Understand the UI

  • On the left hand side: the recipe as it appears on the Guardian. Check it’s a whole recipe!
  • On the right hand side: the translated recipe. This is either by a computer or a human

Step 2
Structure recipe and check!

Your goal: check to see that the right hand side mirrors the left. Keep an eye out for:

  • Ingredient matches
  • Correct quantites
  • Method steps appropriately broken down
  • Accurate timings (...where appropriate – often timing information is missing)
  • Correct picture – you may need to open the original article to verify
TOP TIP If you need to recreate a portion of the recipe from scratch just select the text and use the appropriate keyboard shortcut.

Step 3
Final check then submit

Double check what you’ve done and hit submit. The recipe will move to a different queue for somebody else to check (and then verify)..

Help & FAQ

Keyboard shortcuts?! ⌨️

Yep. Often things don't get parsed correctly first time, so you'll need to do it yourself.

Select some text and press
  • i to add a single ingredient to the last list
  • l to add as new list of ingredients
  • m to add text as a method step

The source recipe doesn't look right

  • If in doubt skip the recipe
  • If in lots of doubt, copy the URL and send to off-platform@@theguardian.com

What are the three stages?

  1. Curation. This is the first pass capture. This is where you'll ensure that the recipe is correct, and will likely require a bit of work.
  2. Verification. You're checking someone else's initial capture. You're making corrections and improving here.
  3. Final Check before submitting for usage! It's really important you're rigorous in checking. Make corrections and submit.
